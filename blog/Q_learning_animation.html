How Q-learning is a stochastic approximation

The classic Q-learning update

Q(s,a) ← Q(s,a) + α [ r + γ maxa′ Q(s′,a′) − Q(s,a) ]

This is exactly the Robbins–Monro stochastic approximation algorithm applied to the Bellman optimality fixed-point equation.

  • θ = Q-values (the parameter we are learning)
  • The target is a noisy Monte-Carlo-style sample of the true expectation
  • α controls step size — too large → divergence, too small → slow convergence
  • Even though transitions are deterministic here, ε-greedy exploration + single-sample bootstrapping introduce stochasticity
  • Over many updates the noise averages out → Q → Q*
